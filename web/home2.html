People have fragmented identities across services and can't carry reputation. This demo shows a phone-held identity, per-service delegate keys, and a QR sign-in flow.

Phone app

Holds your private identity key and issues delegates.

Delegate keys

Per-service disposable keys to reduce risk.

QR sign-in

Scan a QR to authorize a delegate key for a service.

Web-of-trust

People vouch or block keys so signatures are meaningful.

Phone app

Your phone holds the long-term identity key. It creates short-lived delegate keys for each service so you don't give away your master key.

Delegate keys

Delegates are per-service keys signed by your phone. They reduce risk and make key rotation simple.

QR sign-in

Services show a QR that your phone scans. After you approve, the phone signs a delegate and sends it back to the service.

Web-of-trust

People can vouch for or block keys, making signatures meaningful across contexts.

How it works

  • Phone app: holds identity key and vouches for delegates.
  • Delegates: disposable keys per service.
  • QR Sign-in: delegates are authorized by a signature from your identity key.

Prototype — not a production system.
