QR Sign-in

There is no special access between one-of-us.net and nerdster.org. The ONE-OF-US.NET phone app uses the camera to see and read the sign-in parameters in the QR code and then performs an HTTP POST over the Internet to the address it was instructed to, optionally end-to-end encrypted.

Our signed content is published

Any service can read, verify, and leverage the statements you and your delegates have signed and published.
For example: My signed content is here: identity, Nerdster content.

Leverage these to fit your needs:

  • Identity statements so your service can compute who's who and who.
  • Nerdster follow statements so your service can compute who's intresting to whom and for what.
  • And all the rest of it: ratings, comments, equate/relate statements, and so forth.

Your service can do whatever

Your service doesn't need to be as naked and transparent as the Nerdster.

  • It doesn't have to use delegate keys; it might be enough for your service to just identify a Point of View (PoV).
  • It doesn't have to expose folks' content, although this enables other services to audit (which can add responsibility).
