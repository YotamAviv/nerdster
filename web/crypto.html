Hash codes, Tokens

Consider a hash code of data as a keyless signature. A hash is short regardless of the amount of data. That's why we use them as tokens of keys as well as of statements.

Our use

--Keys

  • Identity Layer: You have a public/private key pair; the public key is your identity. You vouch for another's identity (and humanity) by signing a statement that includes their identity (their public key).
  • Delegate Keys: You create and give other services disposable public/private key pairs and use your own, private identity key to sign structured statements stating that these key pairs represent you on those services.

--Tokens

  • A key token is used to form the URL for requesting statements signed by that key over our HTTP interface, specifically: https://export.one-of-us.net/?spec=<key token>
  • Statement tokens
    • We chain all statements signed by a key to prevent back-dating in case a key is compromised. The "previous" field of a signed statement is the token of the previous statement signed by that key.
    • We identify the last valid statement signed by a key that is revoked by that statement's token.
