ONE-OF-US.NET

A network of the people, by the people, for the people. Retis, ergo sum. (I network, therefore I am)

Manual

The one-of-us.net phone app is your digital wallet for identity. It creates, stores, and lets you sign statements with your cryptographic public/private key pairs (a single identity key pair; as many delegate keys as you want).

  • Sign and publish trust/block statements referencing other keys thus forming our cryptographically secure, web-of-trust.
  • Create disposable, delegate key pairs, associate them with your identity, and allow other services (the Nerdster) to state stuff on your behalf.

The statements are replicated to the cloud and are meant to be portable and available to be read and trusted by anyone or any service. For example, my identity statements are here: export.one-of-us.net

Beginner: Join the network

Until you trust someone else on the network, your network will be empty. Until someone else on the network trusts you, you'll be invisible to the network.

Trust someone

Options:

  1. Meet them in person: Tap the big Scan button (QR icon) at the bottom center of the main screen, and scan the QR code on the main screen of the other person's app.
  2. Remote sharing: Use the Share button (bottom left) to send your public key/QR to a friend. Receive theirs in return. View the QR code they sent you on another screen (laptop/tablet) and scan it with your phone using the Scan button.
  3. Copy/Paste: If someone sends you their key as text (JSON), copy it to your clipboard. Open the scanner (Scan button) and look for the Paste icon/button to process the text directly.

Once scanned, the app will initiate a "Trust Statement". Fill in the "moniker" (typically first name) and optionally a "comment", then tap Trust.

Let your associates trust you

Do the reverse of the above. Show them your QR code, or share it via the Share button.

Note: Until someone on the network trusts you, you are effectively invisible to the entire network.

Delegate sign-in to the Nerdster

Method A: On a separate screen (Computer/Tablet)

Access the Nerdster on a computer web browser:

  1. Go to nerdster.org.
  2. Click "QR sign-in" to display the Nerdster's sign-in QR code.

Use your ONE-OF-US phone app:

  1. Tap the Scan button (bottom center).
  2. Scan the Nerdster's QR code on your computer screen.
  3. If this is your first time, the app will ask to create a "delegate key" for the Nerdster. Confirm this. (This authorizes the Nerdster to post on your behalf without exposing your main identity private key).

Method B: On the same device (Phone)

If you are visiting Nerdster on your phone's browser, you can't scan a QR code. Instead, use one of the Magic Links provided on the sign-in screen.

  • Universal Link (https://...): usually works best on iPhones.
  • Magic Link (keymeid://...): usually works best on Android.

Tap the link recommended for your device to launch the app and authenticate seamlessly.

You should now be signed in.

Statement types

Statements are signed by your active ONE-OF-US public/private key pair. The subject is always another public key. The verb defines your disposition:

Form the network: {trust, block}

Your trust network is defined by these relationships.

  • --moniker: Required for trust statements (e.g. "Alice").

Authorize other services: {delegate}

Your identity key is you. Delegate keys are disposable keys you hand to services (like Nerdster).

  • --revokeAt: If a service is compromised, you can revoke its key.

Maintain your identity {replace}

If you lose your phone or key, you can replace it. This links your new key to your old identity history.

Mechanics of using the app

Navigate by swiping left/right or using the Menu button (bottom right) to access the Management Hub.

  • PEOPLE screen: Lists everyone you {trust, block}. Manage these statements here.
  • SERVICES screen: Lists your active {delegate} keys.
  • ADVANCED screen: Options to {replace} your key or view history.

Overwrite or Clear

Your disposition towards another key is singular. If you trust a key and then block it, only the block counts.

Clear acts as an erase. If you "clear" a key, it's as if you never spoke about it.

To change a statement, simply find the person in the PEOPLE screen and edit the relationship.

Novice: Maintain your own keys

When you create replacement keys, you need to ensure your network knows it's still you.

Your identity keys

If you lose your key (phone lost/wiped):

  1. Reinstall the app and create a new identity.
  2. Go to Advanced > Manage Identity History (or similar flow if prompted).
  3. You will need to reach out to friends who trusted your old key and ask them to trust your new one.

Once they verify and trust your new key, the link is re-established.

Advanced: Maintain your network

Blocking

Use sparingly!

Only block if a key is a bot, a bad actor, or hopelessly careless (trusting bots). Do not block people just because you dislike them—that's what content filtering is for.

Help friends recover

If a trusted associate loses their key:

  • Verify their new identity (meet them, call them).
  • Trust their new key.
  • Clear your trust in their old key (optional but clean).

Pitfalls

DO NOT: Block people you dislike

Those are real people. Block them on the application layer (Nerdster) if you don't want to see their posts. Blocking at the identity layer says "This is not a valid human."

DO NOT: Trust via remote QR blindly

If you get a text/email with a QR code saying "Trust me, it's Bob", verify it's actually Bob (call him, recognize his voice). Don't just scan random QRs.
