nerdster — content layer.

NAME

nerdster — a content rating and discovery tool built on the ONE-OF-US.NET identity layer

SYNOPSIS

Reference implementation for the decentralized identity paradigm All your likes are belong to us (us)..

DESCRIPTION

Nerdster is a decentralized web application for discovering, rating, and discussing content (movies, books, recipes, etc.). Unlike traditional social networks, it does not rely on a central authority to curate your feed or manage your identity. Instead, it uses the ONE-OF-US.NET identity layer to establish a Web of Trust.

Users sign their own content statements using cryptographic keys. Nerdster aggregates these statements to build a view of the world that is unique to each user's Point of View (PoV).

TRANSPARENCY

Nerdster is designed to be "naked" data-wise. It does not hide the underlying mechanics of the decentralized web.

  • Verifiable Statements: Every action you take (rate, comment, follow) signs and publishes a cryptographic statement. These can be verified by any service, not just Nerdster.
  • Exposed Plumbing: You can inspect the raw data at any time. Turn on Show Crypto in the settings to see the signed JSON statements for every item in your feed.
  • Service Discovery: Clicking on a key in the node details view reveals where that user's data is being served on the internet.

IDENTITY & SIGN IN

Nerdster does not have its own account system. Identity is managed exclusively by the ONE-OF-US.NET mobile app.

Identity
Access to Nerdster requires an identity (a cryptographic public key).
Delegate Keys
To sign in, use your phone app to create a Delegate Key for the Nerdster's use. You can revoke or disassociate this delegation at any time on your own (using your ONE-OF-US.NET mobile app, not the Nerdster).
Sign In
Desktop: Use the ONE-OF-US.NET app on your phone to scan the QR code displayed on the Nerdster sign-in screen.
Android: Tap the "Magic Link" button on the sign-in page. This uses a custom scheme (keymeid://) to launch the identity app and initiate the delegation process.
iPhone: Tap the "Universal Link" button. This uses a recognized web link (https://one-of-us.net/...) which iOS redirects to the installed ONE-OF-US.NET phone app.
Copy/Paste: Advanced users can copy/paste JSON blobs containing a public key for "identity" and a public/private key pair for the delegate key as "nerdster.org".

POINT OF VIEW (PoV)

Everything in Nerdster is subjective. There is no global truth or global average rating. When you use Nerdster, you are seeing the world from a specific Point of View (usually your own).

The content you see, the ratings you trust, and the tags you browse are all filtered filtered by trust. You only see content from people you trust, or people trusted by people you trust (up to a configured distance).

You can change the active PoV and Context using the controls in the Network Settings Bar. Switching the PoV or Context affects your view immediately, updating the graph, feed, and ratings to match the selected perspective and topic.

FOLLOWING & CONTEXTS

While the Identity Layer handles who is human, Nerdster handles who is interesting. You can follow users for different Contexts.

<nerdster>
The default context is "<nerdster>" which follows everyone you have personally vouched for in the Identity Layer. You can tune this context explicitly (follow or block people in this context) without affecting your real-world identity vouching. This affects the view of those who "<nerdster>" follow you.
Custom Contexts
You can define arbitrary contexts like music, news, family, local, etc... A user you trust for coding advice might be blocked in your politics context. This granular control ensures your feed remains relevant.
Changing Follows
You manage your follows visually using the Graph View. Simply click on a node (representing a user identity) to select it. The side panel will update to show your current relationship. Click Follow, Block, or Clear to update your stance for any context.

CONTENT & VERBS

Users interact with subjects by publishing statements signed by your delegate key.

Like
Thumbs up or down.
Comment
Dismiss
A gentle way to hide content you've already seen or aren't interested in. Dismissals can be set to Snooze, which hides the item until new qualified activity (like a new comment from a friend) wakes it up or for Forever. You can choose to ignore dismissals using a checkbox on the filter bar.
Mobile Swipe Actions
On touch devices, you can Swipe the Image Left to dismiss it Forever (you can add other ratings before hitting publish). Swipe the Image Right to Snooze and Like.
Censor
A harsh action for offensive or dangerous content. Censoring a subject removes it from your view entirely. Crucially, your censorship protects those who follow you—if you censor something, it is hidden from your followers who trust your judgment. You can choose to ignore censorship using a checkbox on the filter bar. Like everything else, censorship is decentralized and is respected based on who you follow in the context you're using.
Relate / Equate
Users can link subjects together. Relate suggests a connection (e.g., "The Matrix" related to "Cyberpunk"). Equate asserts two subjects are the same thing (e.g., two URLs for the same article).

TAGS

Tags are extracted from comments (e.g., #scifi). Clicking on a tag or selecting it in the tags dropdown in the filter bar should show content tagged as such and possibly related content.

FILTERS

The interface provides powerful tools to curate your experience.

Dismissal Filters
Controls whose dismissals are respected.
  • My Disses: Hide things I have dismissed.
  • PoV's Disses: Hide things the current PoV user has dismissed. (Currently not in the UI.)
  • Ignore Disses: Show everything, even if dismissed.
Censorship
By default, censored content is hidden. You can toggle "Show Censored" to inspect what your network has flagged.

SINGULAR DISPOSITION

A user can only have one active disposition toward a subject at a time. If you "Like" a movie at 10:00 and "Dismiss" it at 10:05, the system only respects the Dismissal. The latest statement hides the previous one. A newer comment overwrites the former.
The clear rating action removes your previous disposition, like you never said nothin' at all.

Conflicts between users (e.g., Alice says "Related", Bob says "Not Related") are resolved based on your computed Trust Graph. If you trust Alice more than Bob, you see the relationship.

COMPUTING YOUR NETWORK, SHOWING CONFLICTS

Nerdster's computation of your Web of Trust is neither definitive nor particularly smart. It avoids complex heuristics or voting systems.

Consider the core dilemma: If one of your trusted friends vouches for an identity key (asserting it represents a capable human, acting in good faith), but another friend blocks that same key, no software can definitively know the truth. Other tools might leverage counts or statistical signals to guess, but Nerdster does not.

Instead, Nerdster simply notifies the parties involved. If your associates are in conflict, they'll need to talk to each other and sort it out.
