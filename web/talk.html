What happened?

See Crypto boxes on website
When you scan phones:
  • You cryptographically sign a statement referencing the other person's public key using your own private key.
    • Your private identity key always stays on your phone (unless you export it for use with other software - know what you're doing)
  • That signed statement is published to a location where everything your key has signed can be found.
When you create and sign in with a delegate key, you:
  • Create a new, disposable public/private key pair
  • Sign and publish that this key represents you on that service
  • Securely transmit that key pair to the service
Complete Cryptographic Digital Signature Chain
  • from your public identity key to all the whitelisted content you see.
Show statements and keys on the apps.
  • Statements
  • Keys
  • Raw, interpreted, QR, text
  • Link to published statements
    That's where both apps load data from (and only)
All activities on the apps sign and publish statements
  • Verifiable by anyone or anything.
  • portable, not trusted because of where you found it, but because of who signed it.
Both apps get all of their content from those same published statements.
  • Nerdster and the Identity Layer are not connected, have no special access.

What's next

It can look like the app is the Nerdster and that the ONE-OF-US.NET phone app is how you sign into it, but that isn't the point

  • For starters: Anyone could build services leveraging our identity layer, and that'd be neat
  • Beyond that, the point is that it's decentralized, ours (what else is these days? Are we losing the plot?)

Bottom line: You gotta believe

  1. Get a crypto key, get recognized, why not.. Just in case..
  2. Make yourself seen (do something on the Nerdster, loop someone else in, etc..)
  3. You don't have to over expose - don't censor, don't block, no worries...
  4. Be shy, lie low a little. But if you do believe then don't keep it all to yourself.
