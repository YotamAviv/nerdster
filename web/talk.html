Explain:
When you scan phones:
  • You reference the other person's public key
  • You sign a statement vouching for who he is and that he's not robot using your own private crypto key (that always stays on your phone, unless you export it for use with other software)
  • You publish that signed statement in a location where everything your key has signed can be found.
when you create the delegate:
  • You create a disposable public/private key pair
  • You sign and publish a statement stating that that key represents you on a service
  • You securely transmit that key pair to the service.
Complete cryptographic digital signature chain starting at your key to all the whitelisted content you see.

Both apps get all of their content from those same published statements.
  • Nerdster and ONE-OF-US.NET are not connected, have no special access.
Heterogeneous: Anyone can make a different, better Nerdster leveraging the existing content but better.. Better conflict detection or identity Network, better UI, richer abilities, and that can fragment even further with competing services playing off each other's improvements. Can't do that with our Facebook, X, Insta content..

Your identity is your crypto key. That's the paradigm

  • You can use any tech to sign with it, issue delegates, claim and revoke old keys, vouch for others
  • Folks can be their own identities on any service, not an account at a particular service
  • Folks can follow or otherwise reference identities instead of accounts at a particular service
