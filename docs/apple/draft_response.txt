**Nerdster, ONE-OF-US.NET and decentralized moderation (censorship)**

ONE-OF-US.NET builds your decentralized identity network. 
Any person can vouch that another key represents  someone who is " human, capable, acting in good faith" as well
to block any key as being controleed by a "bot, spammer, bad actor, careless, confused".
Each person can use any service (like the Nerdster) from their own Point of View (PoV).
And so on the identity level, any person can already, right away, block any bad actor. This
will affect their view as well as any other person who trusts their judgment.

The Nerster is the demo / proof of concept app for the affinity and content layer that sits on top of the identity layer.
Any user can follow or block other users for a variety of contexts or block block them completely.
Any user can censor any content for any reason.
Both of these will immediately affect their view as well as other Nerdster users who trust their judgment.

I've added an implicit agreement to the Terms of Service and Safety & Policy on the sign in dialog.

**On the nature of this app's "whitelisted" and specifically invited people and content**

Unlike TikTok or Instagram, where you see content from strangers, the Nerdster only shows you content from people you, or someone you trust, has explicitly vouched for. You always know who posted what — there is a cryptographic signature chain to every piece of content. A stranger cannot post content that reaches you. This fundamentally limits the surface area for objectionable content compared to traditional platforms.

A past concern of yours was that new users who install the app can't sign in without the ONE-OF-US.NET app, and 
so in an earlier release I included a "bootstrap" feature where you use a transient identity that
implicitly trusts me and a transient delegate key so that you can contribute content (like, comment, etc..).
I've rolled that back as it's really a stretch and doesn't fit in with how this is supposed to work.
It'd be like trying to get driving directions without revealing your location.
To replace it, I've added an "Enter the Nerdster!" (use the "+" button on that dialog to see it) choice on the sign in screen where you do see content as me
but can't contribute content. With the previous "bootstrap" feature, contributed content would most likely have been lost and
would not have been visible to anyone as the whole point is to only show folks content from people they trust.
I hope that this is sufficient to reach the App Store as well.
